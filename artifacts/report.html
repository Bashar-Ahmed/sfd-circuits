Safe Flow Decomposition
for Circuit Discovery

Can the "certain core" of a genomics flow problem certify the mechanisms of a transformer? We build the bridge, validate the machinery, and test it on a model whose circuit is known exactly.

Benchmark · MIB / InterpBench IOI Model · 6L·4H SIIT transformer Ground truth · 7-edge circuit Engine validated · 50/50 vs brute force

A transformer's edge-attribution graph — nodes are attention heads and MLPs, edges carry importances, source is the embedding, sink is the logits — is structurally the exact object Safe Flow Decomposition was built for in RNA-transcript assembly: a single-source, single-sink DAG with a scalar per edge. Safe-flow reports the subpaths present in every valid decomposition — the decomposition-invariant "certain core." This report asks whether that certainty transfers to circuit discovery.

01

The object: a circuit is a flow DAG

The MIB InterpBench IOI model is a 6-layer, 4-head transformer trained with Strict Interchange Intervention Training, so its circuit is known by construction. Over 32 nodes and 1108 candidate edges, the ground-truth circuit is just 7 edges. Two of them — input→m0 and a4.h1→logits — are bridges: every input→logits path in the circuit must cross them. Bottlenecks like these are exactly where flow-safety theory expects to have leverage.

02

The bridge & the method

Safe-flow needs a genuine non-negative conserved flow. Raw signed attribution is neither. The fix is a hierarchy: Integrated Gradients' completeness pins only the global value; Layer-wise Relevance Propagation supplies Kirchhoff conservation by construction (AttnLRP extends it through attention). Our pragmatic build takes |EAP-IG| and projects it onto the conservative-flow cone.

Safe-Circuit(G): 1. flow f* = argminf ‖f − |attr|‖² s.t. f ≥ 0, f_in(v)=f_out(v) (Dykstra projection) 2. safety enumerate maximal safe paths: P safe ⇔ excess f_P > 0, f_P = Σ f(e_i) − Σ f_out(v_i) 3. score σ(e) = maxP ∋ e f_P (forced flow through a complete safe route) 4. select rank by σ → MIB greedy circuit → AUROC / faithfulness

Engine validated. The excess-flow test and maximal-safe-path enumeration are checked against brute-force enumeration of all unit decompositions on 50 integral flows (branch, bowtie, bottleneck, diamond, residual-skip + 45 random DAGs): the excess set equals the "in-every-decomposition" set exactly, and the projection restores conservation to ~1e-16. 50/50 pass.

03

Result — the certificate degenerates

On the dense residual DAG, the append rule f_P' = f_P − (f_out(u) − f(u,v)) drives excess negative after one hop, because f_out(u) ≫ f(u,v) at every internal node. Across all three attribution sources, no maximal safe path exceeds length 2, and σ(e) is nearly identical to the projected flow.

Consequently the primary MIB metric is flat. Over 6 bootstrap resamples of the data, the σ re-ranking is statistically indistinguishable from raw attribution (paired Δ: EAP −0.04, EAP-IG +0.01, IFR +0.06 with ±0.2 per-seed swings). Two principled repairs also fail: the min-forced-flow robustness certificate zeros 99% of edges (AUROC ≈ chance), and re-conserving a sparsified backbone re-densifies it.

04

Result — conservation is a bottleneck detector

The degeneracy hides a robust selective effect. Splitting the ground-truth edges into the 2 bridges and 5 branch edges reveals what conservation does: it lifts the bridges by 10–18× in rank while pushing branches down. Local attribution under-credits a bridge because its marginal effect is diffuse; conservation instead assigns it the full forced throughput it must carry.

05

Why genomes, not transformers

Safe Flow Decomposition draws its power from bottleneck structure. An RNA splice graph chains exons with modest branching, so long stretches of flow are forced and safe paths are long and informative. The transformer computational graph is the opposite:

  • No forced multi-hop routing. Residual additivity connects every earlier component to every later one; f_out spreads over dozens of edges, so excess drains at length 1–2. Excess-flow safety measures a topological-bottleneck property the residual stream structurally eliminates.
  • Conservation re-densifies sparsity. Any projected or thresholded backbone is undone by the conservation constraint, which reroutes mass through connecting edges.
  • Safety ≠ necessity. Where σ is defined it certifies invariance of a chosen attribution flow, not the model's causal circuit. The important IOI heads live at high-fan-out hubs — exactly where σ collapses.
The useful reframing. This is a precise negative characterization: it names the graph property (genuine bottlenecks / sparsity) a flow-safety approach to circuit discovery requires, and shows off-the-shelf residual-stream graphs lack it. The most promising direction is to apply safe-flow where the graph is sparse — SAE-feature circuits, QK attention-routing graphs, single-token information-flow graphs — not the dense head/MLP DAG.
06

What to take away

  • The bridge is sound and reusable. attribution → conserved flow → path certificate is mathematically valid; its yield depends entirely on graph sparsity, which is a design knob (granularity, backbone extraction, AttnLRP two-commodity flows).
  • Conservation as a bottleneck primitive is a concrete, cheap win: to recover a circuit's entry/exit edges that attribution under-credits, project onto the flow cone and read off the top-ranked bridges.
  • Excess flow as a soft feature, not a binary certificate, is a legitimate order-independent structural signal even when the strict certificate degenerates.
  • Honesty of scope. σ certifies the attribution field, not the model. The load-bearing validation is causal — ablate high-σ vs flow-matched-random edges — complementary to safety, not replaced by it.
